Google Scholar is a free to use research library utilised by various researchers to gather various forms of academic literature, in which can be used for their literature analysis or as reference in their individual scholarly studies. Simply type in the prompt into the search-box to start exploring the library.

Below every scholarly text shown in the result lies the cite button which generates your citations in many formats


Science Direct is a free to use web library for exploring any scientific, technical, and medical research and scholarly texts contributed by various researchers globally into its wide library.

Only enter the prompt in the search box below.Use the Advanced Search option to filter your prompt based on the article name, author, publication date, and other identifying information about the text you wish to investigate or reference.


Wikipedia is a free online encyclopedia that anyone can edit, and millions already have. Wikipedia's purpose is to benefit readers by presenting information on all branches of knowledge. Hosted by the Wikimedia Foundation, it consists of freely editable content, whose articles also have numerous links to guide readers towards more information. Wikipedia. But there has been backlash regarding the use of this site to reference information.

Although it provides free knowledge and information to the public, it is prone to misinformation and potential propaganda due to its susceptibility to malicious editors. Be careful when using information from this site.
